Identity, Values and Strengths

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

Around 6 years ago I made the decision to make big changes to what I ate. This was a very ethically driven decision as I had come to understand a lot about the world we live in. I knew I loved the earth and all its ecological wonders and I loved non-human animals. This made me think about my own actions and the impact they had, and I felt ethically I wasn’t doing enough. Looking back I am without a doubt glad I did what I did, and at the same time learned a tremendous amount about me, and what I care about.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

Spending time around other like minded and positive people is something I have always held at my core. I have been lucky to share a lot of my social life with friends who have lifted me up and shaped who I am today. Mutual respect is something that means a lot to me and at the centre of my personal culture.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

My biggest strength is my love of learning new skills and I know this will support me as I take on the biggest learning task of my life so far. I also know that working in teams successfully is something I have done before and taken pride in for a long time. On this journey I know that teamwork is so important and I plan to use this skill to excel and progress with like-minded people.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey? 

I am aware that my learning journey thus far hasn’t been what I wanted and it took me along time to understand this. But I feel now at this moment that I am on the right track being here at Dev Academy. I am someone who does struggle with learning difficulties but I know I can plan, work hard, and get through to where I need to be, with confidence in myself.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

At my previous job as a mechanic, there was many times where teamwork was essential to getting work done in the required timeframes. Asking for help was necessary, but other individuals had their own workload. With time pressure being the shoulders of me and my team, there where times when stress shows. In these situations practicing practicing and mutual respect was key. Communication and was also just important so me and my colleagues knew how we were feeling, and what needed to be done if things were off. These strategies often worked well and at other times didn’t. I would definitely say that more planning would of helped in certain scenarios and better communication. I know that I could of held a better mindset at certain points as well and that is something which is at the forefront of what I want to personally improve.
