Tapa Whā

Xavier Kirkpatrick

In this blog, I will use the values of Te Whare Tapa Whā;
The four walls or dimensions of wellbeing, and the roots of these walls,
Whenua, the land, to demonstrate a wellbeing plan to support me on my
path at Dev Academy and throughout daily life.

Return to main

Taha Wairau - Spiritual Wellbeing

I'm not typically a religious person, but I do find myself
deeply connected to spirituality. I often contemplate my place in the world and
the universe at large. To nurture this connection, I will set aside
periods each day for spiritual reflection like this:

- When I wake up, spend five minutes thinking about my purpose.
- Think about my existence and remain thankful for the life I have.
- Be grateful for the people who are in my life and care for me.

Taha Hinengaro - Mental and Emotional Wellbeing

Prioritising my mental well-being holds great significance in my life.
It empowers me to tackle my daily tasks with resilience in the face of
challenges. To ensure I maintain a healthy mentality and emotional state,
I will commit to the following daily practices:

- Ensure I maintain good sleeping habits, at least 6-7 hours of sleep.
- Wake up around the same time each day.
- Physical exercise such as running three times a week.
- Eat small, eat often, mostly plants.
- Engage in positive social interaction often.
- Speak to oneself respectfully, and don’t channel negative, unproductive thoughts.
- Spending a good amount of time each day listening to music.

Taha Tinana - Physical Wellbeing

Staying physically healthy holds immense importance in my life, as it plays
a pivotal role in maintaining proper mental health as well. To uphold my
physical well-being, some of the daily practices I will engage in are:

- Maintaining a healthy sleep schedule.
- Eating good, healthy food through a balanced diet.
- Cardio exercise at least 3 times a week.
- Stretch at the end and the beginning of the day.

Taha Whānau - Family Wellbeing

My connection with my immediate family and friends is a cornerstone of
my life, as I recognise the significance of positive interactions and
regular socialising for my overall well-being. Engaging in conversations and
participating in group activities are key elements in maintaining good mental
health. Some actions I will take to promote connection to family and
strengthen these bonds are:

- Cook dinner and spend time with my immediate family twice a week.
- See friends and engage in social activity once a week.
- Walk my dog at lunchtime every day.
- Watch movies and bond over common interests with my immediate family

Whenua - The Land

My connection to the land and the natural world holds great importance in
my life. Living near water and beautiful park areas has always been a
significant source of mental well-being for me. To nurture this connection
and continue benefiting from it, here are some actions I will take:

- Spend time near and connect with the ocean two or three times a week.
- Swim in the ocean on weekends.
- Leave the city and go for walks and hikes in the bush on weekends when I have time.
- Spend time in the park observing and appreciating nature; the birds, the wind, the trees.

I know utilising these methods will be of great importance on my journey
through Dev Academy, as they ground me and give me mental and emotional
stability and happiness. I took forward to using the Te Whare Tapa Whā model
in everyday life to support all aspects of myself.
