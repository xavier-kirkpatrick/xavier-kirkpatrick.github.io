EQ and IQ

Here i'll talk about the values of both EQ and IQ,
their differences, and what they mean.

Return to main

Most of us are familiar with the term IQ. Intelligence Quotient has been a measure of cognitive
ability and intelligence for a long time, defining someones skill and potential.

With IQ being important on one hand, there is also EQ. The Emotional Quotient is just as important, if not more important than IQ
as it directly defines who you are as a person and how you socially integrate with others around you. It is ones ability to emotionally assess situations
relating to others and be self-aware of ones own current emotional state.

Especially now when employers are looking to hire, EQ is so important as it give a good idea of how this new person
may work with their team and emotionally and socially integrate. They’re looking for individuals who are good at understanding
others emotions and relate to those around them. EQ also plays a large part in people being great leaders,
needing to be emotionally competent to successfully guide others

Another thing to note is that EQ can be grown and nurtured over time with commitment, practice and self-awareness.
There are many resources available online as well as in person for assisting anyone in pursuit of elevating their Emotional Quotient.
