What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?
  • Firstly, I have learnt that historically I have not given enough time to reflect on what I have learned and now understand how important it is as part of the process. Secondly, the core material has taught me to have confidence in my neurological ability to learn, but for this to work substantial effort is needed. Thirdly, I have learned that I need to ask for help a lot more. I don’t do it enough and it puts me in tight places sometimes. I have realised that I can’t figure everything out with the time of have and asking for assistance is so important to make sure I can learn and continue on.
    • What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?
  • I think these values are paramount when working with others. These values are especially important for understanding myself and how I am around the people I am learning with. I think Self-awareness is so important for when knowing to step away from something and take a break which is a huge part of me and my ability to work effectively.
    • What has surprised you the most about the core learning?
  • I think its significance as part of this process has surprised me the most. I never expected it to be at the forefront of all this but I am happy it is. I also didn’t expect to learn so much about the mind and Neuroplasticity but it has done a lot to make me feel more confident in myself.
    • What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?
  • I found actually being able to insert it as a significant part of my routine quite hard due to the volume of work and the difficulties I have had with learning all of the tech aspects. Being able to probe myself and talk about feelings and struggles each week was also challenging.
    • Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?
  • I would not have been able to answer this before foundations but I definitely understand its significance now. I think it's very important to be able to work with others and in a team but also be able to internally support yourself and know what’s needed holistically at any given time. I also understand how very necessary it is to learn effectively through effort and reflection.
    • Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.
  • I don’t consider it a waste of time. I have learned a lot about myself and how I can approach learning in a different manner from what I have been previously taught. I see now, especially from past working environments, the value of working around people who have good EQ skills and how I can use what I've learned to work successfully in teams in my future career.
